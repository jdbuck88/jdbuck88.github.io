Smoky Scotch: A bonfire in the glass.

Scotland is home to about 150 distilleries and only 10-15 of them make peated scotch, or scotch that is smoky in flavor.

The island famous for making the smokiest scotches is the Isle of Islay... which is what we are focusing on here.

The whisky gets the smoky flavor from drying the barley over a peat fire before being milled, mashed, and distilled.

How to taste peated whisky

Glassware is important.

Ideally, use a Glencairn, a Norlan, or a tulip whisky glass when drinking scotch neat. Pour about 30 mL in a glass.

Take your time.

Don't shoot the whisky. Wait to smell the scotch if possible.

Take a small amount into your mouth and hold it there for a moment or two while breathing through your nose.

Roll it around on your tongue and swallow.

Breathe.

Now that you have acclimated... Take a smell.

Can you smell the smoke? The wood? Perhaps vanilla or raisins?

Each smell will come from the process of making the whisky or the maturation in the wood for years.


Finally... it's time to taste the whisky.

Focus on how it feels. Is it soft or aggressive? Oily or thin?

Since this is an Islay Scotch, can you taste the smoke? Is it subtle or pronounced?

Next, try to determine if it is sweet, salty, and/or astringent. Does it feel like wine tannins on the finish or is it more velvety?


There are many flavors that can be found in scotch... but don't focus too much on that if you are new to this.

Lastly, how is the finish? Do the flavors linger or do they dissipate quickly?




So that's it... Nose, mouthfeel, taste, and finish. Cheers!

A little about the magical island of Islay

Islay is the southernmost island of the Inland Hebrides,located off the west coast of Scotland, and is known as "Queen of the Hebrides" (Banrìgh nan Eilean).

It has a total area of 600 km², with just over 3,000 inhabitants, and as many as nine different active distilleries for whisky production.

Accessible only by a 2.5 hour Ferry or tiny prop plane out of Glasgow, and both of those completely dependant on unreliable weather, Islay is relatively hard to get to.


How do you pronounce Islay? Eye-la.

There are 9 active distilleries on Islay.
